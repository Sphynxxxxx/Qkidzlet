Addition Concepts




Learning Targets:


•Illustrates addition as “putting together, combining, or joining sets”.

We use addition to combine sets.
For example:

6 + 3 = 9


The symbol (=) means “equal to”.

The “plus” (+) sign is part of an addition sentence that means “to add to”.

The “sum” is the answer to an addition sentence.

The numbers in our sentence have names.

6 and 3 are called the “addends”

9 is called the “sum”


Remember:


•Addition is about putting sets together or putting more.

•Addends are more numbers added.

•Sum is the answer in addition.




Subtraction Concepts




Learning Targets:

•Illustrates subtraction as “taking away” or “comparing” elements of sets.

•Illustrates that addition and subtraction are inverse operations.

When we subtract, we “take away”. For example:


7 - 4 = 3


We can also think of subtraction as comparing elements of the given sets.

Minus (-) sign is part of the subtraction sentence that means “to take from”.

The symbol that we use for subtraction is minus or “-”.

7 is called the “minuend and 4 is the “subtrahend”

3 is called the “difference”


Remember:


Subtraction is:

•taking away elements of sets;
•comparing sets; and
•the opposite of addition.
